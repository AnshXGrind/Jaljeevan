NATIONAL GREEN TRIBUNAL
Order No. 2024/ENV/45

Subject: Ganga River Water Quality Management and Pollution Control

WATER QUALITY STANDARDS
All riverine water quality parameters shall conform to:
- Dissolved Oxygen: minimum 5 mg/L
- Biochemical Oxygen Demand: maximum 3 mg/L
- Total Suspended Solids: maximum 100 mg/L
- pH: 6.5 to 8.5
- Turbidity: maximum 2 NTU in sanctuary zones
- Heavy metals: within WHO guideline limits
- Fecal coliform: zero tolerance at designated points

MONITORING INFRASTRUCTURE
1. Sentinel-2 satellite imagery analysis every 5 days
2. CPCB automated water quality monitoring stations
3. Real-time turbidity sensors in all zones
4. Acoustic hydrophone arrays for pollution detection
5. Visual inspection by trained monitors twice weekly

INDUSTRIAL COMPLIANCE
- Sewage treatment plants must achieve 95% efficiency minimum
- Zero liquid discharge policy for mining operations
- Hazardous waste must be disposed 50km from river
- Industrial permits contingent on NGT approval
- Annual environmental impact assessment mandatory

SAND MINING RESTRICTIONS
- Commercial mining banned within 500m of river edge
- Mining season: October-April only (non-breeding)
- Maximum 50 tonnes per day per zone
- Dredging depth: not exceeding 2 meters
- No night mining (21:00-06:00 hours)

VIOLATION PENALTIES
- Water pollution: ₹5 lakh per day of non-compliance
- Unauthorized mining: ₹50 lakh + confiscation of equipment
- Discharging toxic waste: ₹25 lakh + criminal prosecution
- False monitoring data: ₹10 lakh + FIR filing

MONITORING AUTHORITY
- CPCB Regional Office (primary responsibility)
- State Pollution Control Board (secondary monitoring)
- NGT-appointed independent auditors
- Local NGO environmental committees
- Fishermen cooperative representatives

Order Date: 2024-01-20
Compliance Deadline: 60 days from order date
Review Period: Every 90 days
Authority: National Green Tribunal Act 2010
