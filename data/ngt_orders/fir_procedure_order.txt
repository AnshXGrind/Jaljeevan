NATIONAL GREEN TRIBUNAL
Order No. 2024/CRIM/12

Subject: FIR Filing Procedures and Evidence Standards for Environmental Crimes

AUTOMATIC FIR PROTOCOL
Environmental violations detected by monitoring systems shall trigger:
1. Automatic notification to District Magistrate
2. Case registration within 24 hours
3. Evidence preservation and documentation
4. Preliminary investigation within 72 hours
5. Chargesheet filing within 30 days

EVIDENCE STANDARDS
Admissible evidence for environmental prosecution:
- Satellite imagery (Sentinel-1, Sentinel-2 analysis)
- Real-time water quality monitoring data
- Acoustic recordings and hydrophone measurements
- Photographic/video documentation
- Independent environmental audits
- Expert witness testimony (CPCB, WII, IIT specialists)
- Computer-generated causal chain analysis

LEGAL SECTIONS FOR MINING VIOLATIONS
- IPC Section 379: Theft of natural resources (3 year imprisonment, ₹500 penalty)
- IPC Section 409: Criminal breach of trust by public servant
- EPA 1986 Section 15: Environmental damage (6 month to 5 years + ₹100,000)
- Wildlife Protection Act 1972: Habitat destruction (1 to 3 years + ₹500-25,000)
- Biological Diversity Act 2002: Unauthorized exploitation
- Local Water Act provisions: Unauthorized water extraction

CASE FILING AUTHORITY
- District Magistrate has primary duty to file FIR
- Customary magistrate court has jurisdiction
- NGT can order suo moto case registration
- Environmental NGOs authorized to file complaints
- CPCB/State PCB can recommend prosecution

INVESTIGATION PROTOCOL
1. Scene investigation within 48 hours
2. Evidence collection and chain of custody
3. Expert consultation with hydrobiologists
4. Causal analysis linking mining to dolphin decline
5. Economic loss assessment (ecosystem services)
6. Penalties based on severity and repeat offenses

COURT PRESENTATION
- Burden of proof: Clear and convincing evidence
- Expert reports from independent laboratories
- Satellite data analysis by certified consultants
- Aquatic biologist testimony mandatory
- NGT order violation constitutes separate charge

PENALTIES FRAMEWORK
First offense: ₹10 lakh to ₹25 lakh fine + 1 year imprisonment
Second offense: ₹25 lakh to ₹50 lakh fine + 2-3 years imprisonment
Repeat offender: ₹50 lakh to ₹1 crore + 3-5 years imprisonment
Corporate liability: Up to 100 times above plus management prosecution

SENTENCING FACTORS
- Extent of environmental damage
- Duration of violation
- Attempts to conceal evidence
- Cooperation with authorities
- Prior environmental violations
- Economic benefit derived

Order Date: 2024-03-01
Effective: Immediately
Authority: NGT Act 2010, Section 15
Review: Every 180 days based on enforcement outcomes
